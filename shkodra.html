Shkodra: Gateway to the North

A full-day journey through Albania's cultural capital — from Rozafa Castle to lakeside serenity, this trip blends nature, history, and local charm.

SHKODRA DAY TRIPS

On this half-day excursion, explore Shkodra, the Albanian cultural capital and one of the oldest cities in Europe. Known for tradition and natural beauty, this city offers a tourist diversity that cannot be found in any other city in Albania: with rivers, lakes, lagoons and mountains that make Shkodra an unforgettable destination in Albania. On this tour you will visit attractions such as Rozafa Castle, Leaden Mosque, Old Town and History Museum.

Highlights

  • Visit Rozafa Castle with its ancient walls dating back to the first millennium BC
  • Explore the Leaden Mosque, a symbol of religious tolerance
  • Stroll through the Old Town with its beautiful classicist architecture
  • Experience Albania's cultural capital surrounded by stunning natural landscapes
  • Visit the History Museum to learn about the city's rich past
  • Witness the stunning natural landscape of lakes and valleys of the Albanian Alps
  • See the confluence of the Buna and Drini rivers

Detailed Itinerary

Your journey begins at 9:00 am towards the town of Shkodra, known for its stunning natural landscape surrounded by the lakes and valleys of the Albanian Alps. The northern Albanian town of Shkodra, located on the lake of the same name, has an eventful history behind it: Romans, Serbs, Venetians, Turks and Austrians determined its fate. Shkodra was one of the most important cities in Albania - the secret capital of Albania.

On a rocky hill overlooking the city, on the Buna and Drini rivers, is the most important monument of Shkoder: The castle of Rozafa. The oldest wall of the castle dates from the first millennium BC, from the time of the Illyrian King Gentius, to the Romans of 168 BC.

After visiting the castle district, you will have the opportunity to explore the Leaden Mosque. You will take a walk through the alleys of the old town with classicist houses. This city is the symbol of tolerance and religious coexistence in Albania. Two mosques, an Orthodox church and a Catholic cathedral enrich the cityscape.

A visit to the historical museum is on the plan. Here you can see archaeological artifacts and historical photographs that will tell you about the different periods of life in the city. After experiencing the rich culture and history of Shkodra, you will return to your hotel.

Duration: Half day (4-5 hours)
Departure Time: 9:00 AM
Includes: Transportation, English-speaking guide, entrance fees to Rozafa Castle and History Museum
Not Included: Meals, personal expenses, hotel transfers
